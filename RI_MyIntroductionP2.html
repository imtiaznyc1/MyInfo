Who am I?

Hi! My name is Imtiaz Rahman and I preside within the city that never sleeps, New York. Currently I am a sophmore at the Bronx Highschool of Science. In my family I am the only child. Sometimes being a only child may feel....boring....but most of the times it's beneficial for me for I can devote learning time without having to worry about a sibling. In addition, my parents are very loving and never let me feel the absence of a sibling. I come from an immagrant background where my parents come from Bangladesh. I am grateful to be here in America for the oppurtunity of a good education. Aside from everything, one thing that I always think about is exactly why my family decided to move from Florida, my birth place, all the way to New York. I understand that there's more oppurtunities in New York, but still what New York doesn't have are clear blue bodies of water with such kind people. But of course, I feel lucky to have my origins from the humble country of Bangladesh. Though it's a small country, there are some special things about the atmosphere which makes it unique. I always miss the warm climate and the FOOD found in Bangladesh. Some can say I have home sickness.



Some may find my personality unique from the crowd. For starters, I don't make use of major social media platforms such as Facebook and Snapchat. Don't mistake me for being anti-social, because I can socialize with others. It's just that I don't see any real needs for social media as of now. Another thing people should know of me is that I truly distaste Apple. Don't get me wrong, Apple can make some precious products. However, I feel like after the loss of Steve Jobs, the company has been lacking innovation. Now they're surviving due to their brand name. In addition, the price point is too high compared to what they're really offering. Fun fact about me is that I can beat box. The story behind how I learned to do it is really random. During middle school, I was used to pen tapping as a beat making method. For some reason it became a habit. Whenever I sat on my desk, I automatically start pen tapping. At one point I didn't feel like pen tapping because it required physicall work. So, I decided to use an alternative which was my mouth. This bad habit of mine turned out to be something usefull.

In addition to beat boxing, I know how to play the guitar. Just like beat boxing, this means of music was self-learned. I believe it was around 6th grade when I found a guitar I had which was built for kids. However, at that time I thought that was a typical guitar. Since I felt useless, and have always wanted to play the guitar, I decided to learn to play it. I went to youtube and searched up how to play an instrumental to a simple song. By doing this, I gradually became flexible with playing. One day my dad heard and actually bought me a really nice guitar from Guitar Center. Unlike beatboxing, I haven't been able to utlilize the guitar to my benefit except pleasure. By looking at this webpage you might have been able to assume that I am deeply in love with cars, which is true. Though I don't know the mechanics behind cars, I do appreciate the craftmanship behind cars. Cars like the Ferrari La Ferrari are simply astonishing for their proportional beauty and speed. Not only the beauty, but the technology that goes behind these machines also intrigue me for their versitality.

Why cars and technology?

You may be wondering, why do I like both cars and technology. Well, cars always fascinate me in terms of design and power. It's such a product where if both design and power are great, then it seems to connect to its driver. Cars like the Ferrari are so well recognized for how well the proportions of the car fit "perfectly" I feel that when advanced technology is incorporated within cars, it enhances this connection with drivers. We see this happening in the real world. For example, Tesla is trying to increase autonomy in the car through things like autopilot. This will be useful because in times of danger this feature can save someone's life. Not only will it save someone's life, but it will lead to a breakthrough in public transportation. We are already seeing companies like Uber trying to incorporate self-driving to pick up customers without a driver. Not only that, but buses, planes, and trains can be imporved by using some of the latest cutting-edge safety features, which will decrease the risk of an accident. One day we might see a world where there is barely any human interaction in terms of traveling. People will simply scan a QR code or ticket and enter an autonmous vehicle to reach his or her destination. However, some people find it a ittle creepy and risky because there is no human controller to make sure the robot or AI doesn't mess up.
